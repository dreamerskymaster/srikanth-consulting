What it covers

Six pillars that make a graduate ready.

Each pillar is built around doing, not listening. Students practise, get feedback, and repeat, the way real skill is formed.

Pillar 01

Professional Excellence

Corporate etiquette, business communication, and presentation skills. How to write, speak, dress, and carry oneself so that competence is visible from the first minute.

Pillar 02

Career Readiness

Resume and LinkedIn that get shortlisted, structured problem solving, group discussions, and mock interviews with real, specific feedback.

Pillar 03

Leadership Skills

Critical thinking, decision making under constraints, and teamwork, practised through tasks that reward judgment, not memorisation.

Pillar 04

Emotional Intelligence

Self-awareness, reading a room, giving and receiving feedback, and staying steady when the stakes and the pressure rise.

Pillar 05

Mindfulness Practices

Focus, clarity, and composure. Practical techniques that convert interview nerves into calm, credible presence, and prevent burnout later.

Pillar 06

Corporate Simulations

Boardroom debates, client conversations, and crisis scenarios, gamified role-play so students have already "been there" before day one on the job.

The journey

Twelve weeks, one arc.

A suggested structure. The sequence is tailored to each cohort's starting point and placement calendar.

Weeks 1–2

Foundation & self-awareness

Baseline assessment, personal goals, and the mindset shift from student to professional. Where do you stand today, and where are you headed?

Weeks 3–4

Communication & etiquette

Business communication, professional writing, and the everyday etiquette that shapes first impressions.

Weeks 5–6

Presence & presentation

Public speaking, structured presentation, and executive presence, rehearsed and recorded for feedback.

Weeks 7–8

Emotional intelligence & mindfulness

Managing pressure, self-regulation, and focus practices that hold up in real interviews and real work.

Weeks 9–10

Leadership & problem solving

Critical thinking, decision making, and teamwork through case-based and simulated challenges.

Weeks 11–12

Corporate simulations & interviews

Full mock interviews, group discussions, and corporate scenario simulations. Final readiness assessment and report.
